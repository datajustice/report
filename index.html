Data Justice
Exploring how open data effects our communities

Our work

Since the City of Detroit launched the Open Data Portal and GO DATA policy initiative in 2015, the Detroit Digital Justice Coalition (DDJC) and Detroit Community Technology Project (DCTP) have been exploring ideas and practices that promote data justice. By data justice, we mean understanding the ways in which open data can be both beneficial and harmful to residents, especially to communities that have been historically criminalized.

This report - Recommendations for Data Justice - is the product of our organizing efforts, participatory research projects and community education initiatives around open data. We present these recommendations to advocate for the adoption and implementation of community-generated policy provisions and to outline strategies for open data stewardship that focus on access, engagement and accountability. Alongside our recommendations, we also share key takeaways from our research and resources for adapting our strategies.

Recommendations for Data Justice

Engage residents offline

Raise awareness of the City's open data efforts through community forums and training workshops.

Listen and respond to users

Prioritize the release of new datasets based on community interest and need.

Define technical terms

Explain field definitions, abbreviations and technical or political lingo used to code data.

Publish data about all City services

Make information about all City services transparent, even for public services that are now privatized.

Protect the people behind the numbers

Share methods for how and why certain open data are anonymized and others are not.

Share intentions

Publicize upcoming data releases, civic app launch dates or community events.

Connect with us

We expect that our recommendations, and the research that has shaped them, will continue to grow and evolve as we move forward with our data justice work. We welcome feedback and your ideas at communitytechnology@alliedmedia.org.

We also welcome contributions, especially to our Resources page. Share links to projects, popular education tools and curriculum for learning about open data, and more by submitting an issue or pull request to our Github repository (requires a Github account) or by email.
